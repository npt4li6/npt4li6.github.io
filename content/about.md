I'm a physics PhD student in the greater Boston area interested in origins-of-life and cell biophysics. I grew up in San Diego and previously studied physics and philosophy at UC Santa Barbara. During my bachelor's degree, I researched novel DNA nanostructures that mimic centrosome self-assembly. Currently, I'm expanding into the biology of fused cells (syncytia). I'd like to investigate the role of cytoskeletal structures in multinucleated cell pathologies. I'm also interested in making science more accessible in how it’s communicated and in who gets to engage with it.

Despite relocating nearly as far across the country from home as possible, I'm grateful to have landed in another lively coastal city. Apart from my academic pursuits, I enjoy photography, cooking, yoga, and ceramics. I love finding new music and trying out new food.
