Do whatever you want to this recipe. I think it's nice because it's hard to mess up

---

Ingredients
- [ ] ¾ lb pasta (pick a good shape)
- [ ] 1 lb sausage or seasoned ground meat
- [ ] Spinach (as much as you desire – can replace with any veg, preferably green – may change cooking time)
- [ ] ¼ - ½ Cup (at least) Pesto 
- [ ]Parmesan (or similar)
- [ ] ~¼ cup Heavy cream (or half & half)
- [ ] few cloves of Garlic
- [ ] 1-2 Shallot OR Half Onion
- [ ] Salt / Pepper to taste
- [ ] Olive oil
- [ ] Reserved pasta water

---

Instructions
Set large pot of water to a boil.
Prepare the aromatics and vegetables.
	Mince (or press) the garlic. Finely dice the shallot or onion.
***If using a vegetable other than spinach (e.g., broccoli, zucchini, or kale), wash and chop it now into pasta-sized pieces.
Cook the sausage.
	Heat a large pan (big enough to hold the finished pasta) over medium to medium-high heat. Add about 1 tablespoon olive oil to coat the pan.
	If the sausage is in casings, slice lengthwise. Remove casings while dropping into pan. Break it up into pasta-sized crumbles or bits as it cooks.
Add aromatics.
	When the sausage is about 75% cooked, push it to the outer ring edge of the pan and reduce heat to medium or medium-low.
	If the pan looks dry, add about 1 teaspoon olive oil, then add the garlic and shallot/onion and cook for 30 seconds to 1 minute, until fragrant. Stir everything together to combine.
Add the greens.
	Roughly chop the spinach into small shreds and add it to the pan. Turn off the heat and let it wilt.
***If using an alternative vegetable, add it at this stage, sauté until cooked through, then turn off the heat.
Cook the pasta.
	Once the water is boiling, salt it generously (about 1 teaspoon or a large pinch). Add the pasta and set a timer for slightly before the package’s al dente time.
Reserve pasta water and combine.
	When the pasta is nearly al dente, reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water. Drain the pasta and add it directly to the pan with the sausage and vegetables (heat still off).
Build the sauce.
	Add the pesto and mix to coat the pasta. Add splashes of heavy cream (or alternative), stirring to combine. Use small amounts of reserved pasta water as needed to loosen the sauce.
***If the pan has cooled too much, turn the heat on low.
Finish with cheese and seasoning.
	Grate cheese directly into the pan and mix until incorporated, adding more pasta water if needed. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
Serve.
	Transfer to bowls and top with additional grated cheese.
